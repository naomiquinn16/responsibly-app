The ethics of AI is a critical challenge for the global tech industry. The speed with which AI is changing the world has urged the industry to incorporate ethical considerations into technology design, development, deployment and use before being brought to market. But who is truly responsible for ensuring these new technologies are ethical? Is it the data scientist who builds and monitors the AI model or the lawyer who signs off on its release to market? Or ... is it both?

In the infancy of age of AI, law has to be (in a way applied and enforced) with a certain agility so as not to not impede the innovation of technology. And, with many companies lacking even a basic framework for grappling with how their AI products should be designed, built and used responsibly, no one really wants to push the red button for fear of the detrimental consequences that could ensue.

So this is why we built Responsibly.
